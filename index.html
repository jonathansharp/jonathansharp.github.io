About Me

Hello! I am a marine biogeochemist with a particular interest in carbon cyclimg and ocean acidification. Currently, I am a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Washington Cooperative Institute for Climate, Ocean, and Ecosystem Studies (CICOES) and I work in conjunction with NOAA's Pacific Marine Environmental Laboratory (PMEL).

My research focuses on the biogeochemistry of the California Current Large Marine Ecosystem (CCLME). The CCLME is one of four major Eastern Boundary Current Upwelling systems in the world ocean. These systems exist off the west coasts of major landmasses and support high biological productivity and biodiversity. The CCLME in particular is important for a variety of commercial fisheries. However, changes to the Earth's climate are resulting in rapid warming, acidification and deoxygenation in the CCLME. For my work, I am using measurements from robotic profiling floats to quantify how these environmental changes are affecting biological production and carbon cycling in the region.

Some of my previous work has focused on developing and advancing novel methods for measurements of dissolved carbon dioxide in seawater and on using numerical modeling and laboratory experimentation to improve consistency between measurements and calculations. I performed this work while pursuing my PhD from the University of South Florida College of Marine Science, which I earned in 2020.
